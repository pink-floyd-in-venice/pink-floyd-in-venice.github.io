Conceptual Model

In this conceptual model, we linked the theoretical model to existing standards, allowing us to formalize the identified relationships. In general, we tried both to highlight the specifics of each kind while also maintaining consistency with respect to some significant ontologies (especially Dublin Core). While building the model we made some distinctions regarding the kind of metadata needed to sufficiently and extensively describe the resource or entity (* signals a possible unicum in the use of the ontology).
In addition to the widely known DC/DCterms, we employed EBU, Schema.org, CIDOC CRM, Dbpedia-OWL and FRBRER to represent the features of our items.

We came up with four different categories and eight total specifications:

  • WHO: People, Band
  • WHERE: Location
  • WHEN: Date/Feast Day
  • WHAT: Artefacts, Texts, Multimedia, Events

This is the general specification we adopted for describing our resources. It provides a generic framework identifying the resource and its specific field through the use of different existing ontologies.

The description of bands had to take advantage of different frameworks, as a single ontology was simply not specific enough. After researching the domain we came up with the following scheme.

Question Predicate Origin
What is the band’s name? rdfs:label RDF
Who are the members? dbpedia-owl:bandMember Dbpedia-OWL
Who are the former members? dbpedia-owl:formerBandMember Dbpedia-OWL
When did the band form? mo:activity_start Music Ontology
When was the band active? gndo:periodOfActivity GND Ontology
Where did the band form? schema:foundingLocation Schema.org
What is the type of entity? rdf:type RDF
What genre does the band play? schema:genre Schema.org
What albums did they produce? mo:produced Music Ontology

A crucial requirement for us was the description of dates not only from the perspective of the format, but also from the point of view of regional festivities, as the Redeemer's Feast has a different date each year. We made use of the following descriptors.

Question Predicate Origin

People description had to take into account the peculiar properties of musicians as well as traditional "person" descriptors. We thus opted for integrating role and main instrument as key properties.
